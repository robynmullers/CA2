History

Electric Picnic first launched in 2004 for 10'000 people in Stradbally Hall, Laois, Ireland after being founded by Robbie Butler and John Reynolds. Since 2004 it's been voted the Best Big Festival at the Irish Festival Awards. The fesitval takes place each year at the end of August and lasts until the start of September displaying music genres of indie, dance and alternative music. It's most historical acts include Sex Pistols, Bjork, The Stooges, Arcade Fire, The Chemical Brothers and Nick Cave.

The festival originally began as a one day only festival long before it became a weekend long festival. Electric Picnic promotes eco friendly initiatives and quality attractions such as the Body and Soul arena, a 24-hour cinema tent, a silent disco, the comedy tent etc. The event has been described as "award-winning, enormously successful" and an "established brand".

At our first festival in 2004 camping facilities weren't organised however a number of groups of people began to camp out overnight in the designated car park and explored the woods area. This was the beginning of Electric Picnic's chilled out and relaxed reputation and atmosphere. The headline act for our first year was 2 Many DJs while other acts included Grand Master Flash, Jurassic5, David Kitt, Mylo, Soulwax, Plump DJs, Super Furry Animals, Groove Armada and Arrested Development.
